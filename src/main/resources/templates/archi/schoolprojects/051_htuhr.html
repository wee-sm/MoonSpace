How to use Han-river

Of all this time using delivery services, have you thought about the meaning of several layers of packaging? This project began from a paradox of several layers of packaging. This research is confined to areas in Seoul, Korea, and consists of investigating distribution flow, recognizing the problems and suggesting for improvements. Furthermore, it contains architectural feasibility analysis and solution design, based on the prior research. First, collected data of 5-years distribution and displayed in diagrams. As analyzing the quantity of domestic carbon emission and the change of each industrial distribution flow, I was able to observe the following problems.

  • The abnormal volume of the distribution caused by industrial unbalances between Seoul and the other regions. (Seoul is only consuming in the distribution of goods)
  • The cost of the energy consumed by distribution surpasses the cost of the products.
  • The plethora of stock produced by the logic of the market.

In conclusion, the efficiency of energy and control of stocks were in need.

Second, when I compared the volume of distribution in Seoul with other regions, most distributions with Seoul were created by areas adjacent to Seoul. So, improvements in the distribution system between those areas could bring the most effective results. In this regard, I made a hypothesis that using Han-river could be the solution to these problems because Han-river is linked with all the targeted areas.

To evaluate the feasibility of this hypothesis, I selected the products which can be transported by shipping in containers and calculated the volume of the distributions on land that could be substituted by water transportation. In this process, I referred to references in similar cases implemented in German and America. As a result, I drew out the following conclusion.

If the water transportation is capable of utilizing three R/S ships (250 TEU) and ten cargo ships a day, this hypothesis is feasible

Considering the water-way from the Yellow Sea and the space required for docking ships, I selected Ichon-Hangang park as a site for a waterfront port. The site is approximately 2km in length and 150m in width, approximately 3 times bigger than the left image.

Followings are some unique features of the designed port, which separate it from the others.

  • The port applied the mobility from the pontoon system to prepared for the annual flood of the Han-river in the Summer.
  • The unit size of storage yard has been adjusted based on human activities, instead of containers.
  • The traffic system on the port includes both roads for pedestrians and trams in order to improve the approach of users.
  • The crane system connects the entire yard to extend the movable range for containers.

In this project, I did not plan the shape of the port, but the system of the port. I wanted to talk about visions and possibilities of the port. of this port. it was actualized through the next process.

For extending the meaning of the distribution in the port, I adjusted the details of the plan. First, the layout and size of the unit yards were modified to embrace the various programs. Through this modification, the port can afford to operate for human activities.

Moreover, I created a new foundation system using steel frame and wire to satisfy the safety standard for a human. This design was developed from a fixing system of a vessel ship. It was focused on the stability rather than the flexibility of the space.
