There was an old structure that was roled as a working place for near residents. In this place, they could make their future and look after their family in the past 70-years. there was a place not only for work but also for life. It has stood with the citizens for so long. No.111
CheongJu Culture Factory
The revival of the cigarette factory, which functioned as a workplace for nearby residents, was one of the long-awaited projects for the Cheongju government and citizens. Cheongju urban regeneration was a project that transformed an old cigarette factory facilities into a cultural & commercial complex for local citizens and tourists. Our team played a vital role in this project; remodeling the main building of the factory that has an area of 50,000 square meters.

This project had a complex client organization. The government and local civic groups had the proprietary right to the culture space, and the marketing design company and the construction company had the ownership of the commercial space. In this condition, the key of the project was coordinating each space according to the needs of clients, creating a colossal complex building, and preserving the heritage of the symbolical structure.

To be a new place

To be a new place for people lots of things in the structures that consisted the factory should be changed or removed. Than inner space of the factory had gone through major changes to meet the new program. For example, the working spaces used for cigarette making machines was transformed into the central atrium with natural light by removing slabs of several floors. Also, the old smokestack became the symbol of the factory for visitors.

We had to throw away most of the remnants, it was inevitable. But we tried to maintain the special items in our design that represented the factory. So, We carry out the project with respect to the old factory that was the place to live for citizens.

Keep & Break
Facede Design Strategy
 We focused on keeping the massiveness of the old factory while modifying the facade materials based on the function of each space. To do that, we preserved as much of the original facade since the old elevation possessed the grand image of the factory.

First, the pattern from the window positions was kept in the original place, so that the local residents could recall the shape of the old factory. Moreover, we concentrated curtain wall glasses at the lower parts of the facade to maintain the solidness of the original at the upper facade. Despite the opposition from the MD plan, we managed to talk through the benefits and was able to position the glasses as low as possible. Lastly, we kept the uniformity of the facade form inevitable additional openings for machines, galleries, etc. by the 'opening brick wall' as shown on the left.
Work scope as an Project Architect

Our team was made up of 6 manpower Including myself. I worked as a project designer and was the third person in charge of the project, from the Schematic Design to the Construction Administration. Followings are the duties I performed:

  • Assisted MD planners by providing information and suggestions to improve marketing design and feasibility consultation for the commercial environment analysis.
  • Managed the fast-track schedule for 'The Cheongju Crafts Biennale'.
  • Created architectural drawings and documentation for the building permit, environmental impact assessments (including traffic) and value engineering.
  • Supported the construction site by creating documents after reviewing the quality and suitability of the construction and materials used.

This project had a number of unique circumstances. First, the summary table of the entire factory complex had been disordered by several preceding remodeling projects and had to go through extensive modification. Second, the schedule was rigid with no delays allowed, because of the upcoming 'The Cheongju Craft Biennale' is being held in the place. Third, the design needed numerous adjustments in order to satiate the repeatedly altered marketing strategy. Throughout the project, tactful communication skills and practical experiences in urgent situations were greatly required.
